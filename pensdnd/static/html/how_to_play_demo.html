How to Play the Demo

Graciously, the folks over at Foundry included a demo mode for you to try out. It only lasts 30 mins, after which it resets. I'm going to go rules-lite on this, and focus just on how to use the interface.

1. Select the name of the character you want to play. To keep things simple, I just named them after their classes, "Wizard" is a wizard, "Rogue" is a rogue, etc. No passwords necessary.

2. To target an enemy (or an ally), mouse over them and hit "T" on your keyboard. Hit "T" again to untarget. You can target more than one character at a time, depending on the ability.

3. Click the button on your action bar corresponding to your action.

4. If your action is an attack, it will break down into two kinds: a hit, or a save.

4a. If your action is a HIT, you roll, and you're trying to beat your target's armor class. If you do, the computer will say that you hit. Then you get to hit the damage button. If you don't hit, the computer will say miss. Then you don't hit the damage button.

4b. If your action is a save, your target rolls, not you. And this next part is confusing, I know: you want the results of the target's roll to be "Failure". You don't want the target to "Succeed". Because remember, it's not you that's succeeding; it's the target.

That's probably enough for now. If you like playing and want to join up, try filling out an application, or pestering someone to DM a new group!
