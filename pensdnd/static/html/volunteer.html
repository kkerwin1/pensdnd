How to Volunteer

Depending on your interests and skills, there are potentially inumerable ways to contribute, and far more than anyone can think of. The following immediately come to mind. However, if you have other skills, by all means, reach out to Arvon or another DM, and we can dream together about putting them to use!

Play!

The first and most-important way to contribute to the community is to play with us. Playing D&D and other tabletop fantasy games is such a fulfilling and satisfying experience. There's strategy. There's story lines. There's drama and literature. Hell, done right, D&D might be an effective and fun way to homeschool children.

But D&D is for more than just kids. It is, in fact, seeing quite a resurgence in popularity among the 20-40 age group, especially with internet hits like Critical Role sparking curiosity. So, come see what the buzz is about, and join a game.

Map Drawing

Probably the most time-consuming aspect of DM prep is the creation of detailed dungeon maps. If you like drawing, placing sprites, and imagining how a location might look, this might be an appealing hobby for you.

Due to the sensitive nature of this role, it should only be done by players in games other than the ones that they actively play.

Coding

This little project runs on code. More-specifically, it runs on HTML, CSS, JS, Python, Django, PostgresQL, and Linux. But code can only do so much without people to maintain and update it.

If you happen to have some of these skills, or would like to learn, the project could use your help.

Graphic Design

Arvon, unfortunately, lacks any skill in the visual arts. If you're able to create logos and accompanying art to take a website to the next level, then we certainly have need of your abilities!

Becoming a DM

In listening to and watching the guild, my honest impression is that there is more demand to play tabletop games than is able to be met by a single recurring session of D&D. Moreover, being a DM is quite rewarding, as well. For more information, see Be a DM.
